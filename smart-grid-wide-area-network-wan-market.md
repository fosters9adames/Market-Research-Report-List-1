<p><p>The Smart Grid Wide Area Network (WAN) market comprises two main types: Wired WAN and Wireless WAN. Wired WAN utilizes physical connections, such as fiber optic or copper cables, to facilitate high-speed data transfer and reliability in smart grid communications. In contrast, Wireless WAN relies on radio frequencies and cellular technology, offering flexibility and easier deployment in remote areas. Both types support smart grid infrastructure by enabling real-time data exchange, enhancing grid management, and improving reliability and efficiency in energy distribution.</p></p>
