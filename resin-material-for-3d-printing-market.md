<p><p>In the 3D printing market, resin materials are primarily categorized into liquid resin and solid resin. Liquid resins are photopolymer materials that cure when exposed to UV light, commonly used in stereolithography (SLA) and digital light processing (DLP) printers. They are favored for their precision and detail in creating intricate models. Solid resins, on the other hand, are typically used in more durable applications, offering benefits like strength and stability, making them suitable for functional prototypes and end-use parts in various industries.</p></p>
