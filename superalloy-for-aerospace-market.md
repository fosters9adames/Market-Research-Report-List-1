<p><p>The aerospace superalloy market comprises three primary types: iron-based, nickel-based, and cobalt-based superalloys. Iron-based superalloys are primarily used for structural components due to their good mechanical properties and cost-effectiveness. Nickel-based superalloys are essential for high-temperature applications, particularly in turbine engines, due to their excellent oxidation resistance and strength. Cobalt-based superalloys offer high thermal stability and corrosion resistance, making them suitable for specific aerospace components exposed to extreme environments, such as turbocharger rotors and combustion chambers.</p></p>
