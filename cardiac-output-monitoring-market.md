<p><p>The cardiac output monitoring market is segmented into three device types: invasive, minimally-invasive, and non-invasive devices. Invasive devices involve direct measurement through catheterization, providing precise data but carrying higher risks. Minimally-invasive devices offer a balance, utilizing techniques like transformed echocardiography or bioimpedance, which reduce patient discomfort and risk while maintaining accuracy. Non-invasive devices, such as wearable sensors, use external monitoring techniques, ensuring patient safety and comfort but may sacrifice some measurement precision. Each type serves distinct clinical needs.</p></p>
